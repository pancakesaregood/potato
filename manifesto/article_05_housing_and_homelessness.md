# Article 05: Housing and Homelessness

## Summary

This article treats safe housing as a core social foundation and sets a direction for measurable reductions in homelessness.

## Principles

- Housing policy should focus on stability, affordability, and prevention.
- Homelessness responses must combine shelter, health, and housing supports.
- Federal spending should reward long-term housing outcomes, not short-term optics.

## Policy Mechanisms

- Set national housing targets linked to affordability, supply, and supportive housing.
- Support housing-first partnerships with provinces, territories, municipalities, and Indigenous governments.
- Create shared public reporting for homelessness prevention, shelter exits, and permanent placements.

## Measurable Outcomes

- Chronic homelessness declines across reporting cycles.
- Permanent supportive housing stock grows year over year.
- Housing affordability indicators improve in target communities.
