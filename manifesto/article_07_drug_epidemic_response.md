# Article 07: Drug Epidemic Response

## Summary

This article treats the drug epidemic as a public health emergency that requires prevention, treatment, and harm reduction.

## Principles

- People with substance-use disorders deserve treatment and dignity.
- Emergency response should be guided by evidence and local health realities.
- Public safety improves when health systems intervene earlier.

## Policy Mechanisms

- Expand rapid access to treatment, recovery supports, and mental health care.
- Support harm-reduction services where overdose risk is severe.
- Coordinate federal data, procurement, and emergency funding for toxic-drug response.

## Measurable Outcomes

- Overdose deaths decline in participating regions.
- Treatment wait times are reduced and publicly tracked.
- More people remain connected to recovery services after first contact.
