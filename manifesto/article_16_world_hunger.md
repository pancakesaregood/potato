# Article 16: World Hunger

## Summary

This article positions food security as part of Canadian international responsibility and resilient domestic supply planning.

## Principles

- Hunger is a systems problem involving conflict, poverty, logistics, and climate risk.
- Canada should support food security through partnerships, not paternalism.
- Domestic waste reduction and global solidarity can reinforce each other.

## Policy Mechanisms

- Support international food-security partnerships, school nutrition efforts, and resilient agriculture programs.
- Reduce food waste through procurement reform, recovery networks, and reporting incentives.
- Align development finance with local storage, transport, and crop resilience capacity.

## Measurable Outcomes

- Food waste recovery and reduction metrics improve year over year.
- Canadian-supported partnerships report stable delivery and nutrition outcomes.
- Policy reporting shows how domestic and international food actions connect.
