# Article 17: Resource Nationalization

## Summary

This article establishes a framework for public-interest intervention in strategic resources where private control fails the national interest.

## Principles

- Strategic resources should be governed for long-term public benefit.
- Any nationalization pathway must respect law, due process, and fair compensation rules.
- Resource policy should align with climate goals and Indigenous rights.

## Policy Mechanisms

- Create a transparent review framework for strategic public ownership or public-interest stakes.
- Define criteria for intervention based on national security, public revenue, and supply resilience.
- Require Indigenous consultation and public reporting before major ownership transitions.

## Measurable Outcomes

- Strategic resource decisions are justified against published criteria.
- Public returns, domestic processing capacity, and resilience indicators are tracked.
- Any intervention shows documented consultation and legal review outcomes.
