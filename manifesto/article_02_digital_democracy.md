# Article 02: Digital Democracy

## Summary

This article sets the direction for secure, accessible, and auditable public participation through digital tools.

## Principles

- Digital participation should expand democracy rather than replace human deliberation.
- Identity, privacy, and accessibility must be designed together.
- Remote participation should be available across geography and income levels.

## Policy Mechanisms

- Create secure digital consultation and voting systems with published audit logs.
- Support hybrid assemblies for riding, regional, and national decision-making.
- Adopt accessibility and bilingual standards for all core civic interfaces.

## Measurable Outcomes

- Participation rates increase for remote and rural members.
- Digital voting systems produce auditable records without exposing private ballots.
- Accessibility defects are tracked and resolved within published service windows.
