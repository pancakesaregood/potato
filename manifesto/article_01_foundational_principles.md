# Article 01: Foundational Principles

## Summary

This article defines the base political values of the PPPC and provides a stable frame for future policy work.

## Principles

- Human dignity, fairness, and democratic participation are non-negotiable.
- Federal policy should be practical, evidence-informed, and regionally balanced.
- Party governance should remain transparent, reviewable, and open to amendment.

## Policy Mechanisms

- Publish a public principles register that every major policy proposal must reference.
- Require plain-language rationale notes for manifesto and governance changes.
- Run a recurring foundational review at each regular national convention.

## Measurable Outcomes

- All major policy files cite their governing principle set.
- Convention review of foundational principles occurs on schedule.
- Members can trace policy proposals back to published principles.
