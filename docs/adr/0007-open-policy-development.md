# ADR-0007: Allow Any Member to Submit Policy Proposals

| Field | Value |
| --- | --- |
| Status | Accepted |
| Date | 2026-03-15 |
| Deciders | Founding National Convention |
| Affected components | `/proposals/`, proposal web interface, CI/CD pipeline, membership bylaw |

---

## Context

Political parties vary significantly in how they handle the origination of
policy proposals. The dominant models are:

**Top-down:** Policy is drafted by the leader's office, communications team,
or a small policy committee and presented to members for ratification or
feedback. Members have influence at the margins but do not originate policy.

**Delegate-driven:** Policy originates from recognized bodies — riding
associations, commissions, caucus — that have standing to bring proposals
to convention. Individual members participate through their riding or
affiliated body, not directly.

**Open submission:** Any member in good standing can submit a policy proposal,
subject to a defined intake and review process. The proposal's merit determines
its progress, not the proposer's institutional position.

The PPP's governance-as-code architecture (ADR-0001) makes open submission
technically feasible at low operational cost: a member submitting a policy
proposal is structurally equivalent to a developer opening a pull request.
The CI/CD pipeline (ADR-0003) provides the same quality gate for a proposal
from a first-time member as for one from a party officer.

The architectural question is whether open submission introduces risks —
proposal flooding, low-quality submissions consuming review capacity,
coordinated policy capture by organized factions — that outweigh the
democratic benefits.

---

## Decision

We will allow any Member in Good Standing to submit a policy proposal
directly to the PPP governance repository, subject to the following
structure:

### Submission Requirements

Every proposal must include:

1. A completed proposal form (generated by the web interface or from
   `/proposals/TEMPLATE.md`)
2. A minimum 200-word rationale
3. Endorsement from at least 10 other Members in Good Standing, or from
   1 recognized Riding Association
4. Explicit identification of the policy domain and the specific provision
   being proposed or amended

### Intake Gate (Automated)

The CI/CD pipeline validates the submission format and membership credentials
automatically. Proposals that fail intake validation are returned to the
proposer with specific remediation guidance. Intake failures are not publicly
logged as rejected proposals — they are treated as incomplete submissions.

### Rate Limiting

To prevent proposal flooding:

- Individual members: maximum 3 active (not yet voted on) proposals at any time
- Riding associations: maximum 5 active proposals at any time
- Proposals that are substantively identical to a proposal voted down in the
  previous 12 months are rejected at intake with a mandatory 12-month
  cooling-off period

### Prioritization

The Policy and Convention Committee ranks proposals for convention agenda
inclusion based on:

- number of endorsing members (above the minimum threshold)
- cross-domain impact (proposals affecting multiple policy areas are prioritized
  for early review)
- constitutional sensitivity (proposals touching governance structures are
  prioritized)

Ranking criteria are published and version-controlled. The Committee cannot
exclude a proposal from the convention agenda on substantive grounds — only
on procedural grounds (failed intake, active duplicate, cooling-off period).

### Transparency

All proposals — including those that fail intake — are publicly logged in
`/proposals/registry.json` once they have been formally submitted (i.e., not
during drafting). A proposal's full pipeline history is permanently public.

---

## Consequences

### Positive

- Policy can originate from any member, not just those with institutional
  standing. This lowers the barrier to substantive participation.
- The pipeline applies the same quality standards to all proposals regardless
  of origin. A proposal from a party officer does not receive preferential
  treatment over one from a first-time member.
- Endorsement requirements create a light peer-review layer before the formal
  pipeline, filtering proposals that have no traction in the membership.
- Open submission creates a public record of what the membership cares about
  even for proposals that do not ultimately pass, which is valuable for
  understanding member priorities.

### Negative

- Open submission increases proposal volume. The Policy Committee, AI
  pipeline stages, and Constitutional Review Panel all face higher throughput
  demands than a delegate-only submission model would generate.
- Rate limiting and cooling-off periods, while operationally necessary, reduce
  the openness of the model. A member with a genuine urgent proposal who has
  exhausted their concurrent limit is blocked from submitting.
- The endorsement requirement may be gamed by organized factions who coordinate
  endorsements for proposals aligned with their agenda. This is a feature of
  factional competition, not a bug — but it should be monitored.

### Neutral / Watch Items

- The 10-member endorsement requirement is a threshold designed to filter
  completely isolated proposals while remaining accessible. It should be
  reviewed after the first two convention cycles to assess whether it is
  calibrated correctly.
- The prioritization criteria used by the Policy Committee must be auditable.
  Any change to the prioritization formula is a governance document change
  and is subject to the full pipeline.
- Non-member citizens who wish to propose policy have no direct submission
  pathway under this model. A public consultation mechanism (submissions
  visible to the Policy Committee but not entering the member pipeline) may
  be appropriate as a separate track.

---

## Alternatives Considered

### Alternative 1: Riding Association Sponsorship Required

Description: Individual members may not submit proposals directly. All
proposals must be sponsored by a recognized Riding Association.

Reason rejected: This re-introduces institutional gatekeeping into a system
designed to minimize it. Members in ridings with inactive or captured
associations would have no effective proposal pathway. It also concentrates
the policy agenda in the hands of a relatively small number of riding
executives, which is inconsistent with the party's member-driven design
principles.

### Alternative 2: Officer-Only Proposal Rights

Description: Only National Council members, commission chairs, and
parliamentary caucus members may submit proposals for convention consideration.

Reason rejected: This is the standard top-down model described in the context
section. It is explicitly incompatible with the PPP's founding design
principles and the governance-as-code model's premise that governance
legitimacy comes from transparent, accessible process rather than
institutional position.

### Alternative 3: Unlimited Open Submission (No Rate Limiting or Endorsement)

Description: Any member can submit any number of proposals with no
endorsement requirement. All proposals enter the full pipeline.

Reason rejected: Without rate limiting and endorsement requirements, the
pipeline becomes a denial-of-service attack surface. A coordinated group
could flood the pipeline with proposals to consume review capacity and block
or delay legitimate governance work. The endorsement requirement is a minimal
filter, not a substantive gate.

---

## Future Revisions

This ADR should be revisited if:

- Actual proposal volume in the first two convention cycles is significantly
  higher or lower than anticipated, suggesting the rate limits and endorsement
  threshold need adjustment.
- The Policy Committee's prioritization process is found in practice to
  systematically disadvantage proposals from specific member demographics
  or regions, requiring the prioritization formula to be revised.
- A separate public consultation track is established, which may affect
  the intake requirements for member proposals (if public input is already
  incorporated upstream, some member submission requirements may be redundant).
- The endorsement system is demonstrably gamed by coordinated factions in
  a way that distorts the proposal queue, requiring additional filtering logic.
