## 1. Purpose

This bylaw governs the recognition, powers, duties, and accountability of local
Riding Associations, also known as Electoral District Associations.

## 2. Recognition

### 2.1 One Association per Riding

The Party shall recognize no more than one Riding Association for each federal
electoral district, subject to transitional arrangements during redistribution.

### 2.2 Conditions of Recognition

A Riding Association is recognized when it:

- adopts a local constitution consistent with Party governing documents
- elects a local executive
- registers a financial officer and banking information where required
- maintains at least 15 Members in Good Standing in the riding unless waived for
  organizing reasons
- agrees to national data, branding, and compliance standards

### 2.3 Transitional Organizing Committees

Where no Riding Association exists, the National Council may appoint an interim
organizing committee for up to 12 months to recruit members and hold a founding
meeting.

## 3. Membership and Meetings

### 3.1 Local Membership Body

All Members in Good Standing whose official address lies in the electoral
district are members of the Riding Association.

### 3.2 Annual General Meeting

Each Riding Association shall hold an annual general meeting at least once every
15 months.

### 3.3 Notice and Quorum

The annual general meeting requires at least 14 days' notice. Quorum is the
lesser of 10 members or 20 percent of local membership, but never fewer than 5
members.

### 3.4 Executive Meetings

The local executive shall meet at least quarterly and keep minutes.

## 4. Local Executive

### 4.1 Required Officers

Each Riding Association shall elect at least:

- a President
- a Secretary
- a Treasurer

### 4.2 Recommended Additional Officers

Local constitutions should also provide for:

- an Organizing or Membership Chair
- a Policy Chair
- a Youth Representative
- an Equity and Accessibility Representative

### 4.3 Terms

Local executive officers serve two-year terms and may be re-elected. No local
President may serve more than three consecutive full terms without a one-term
break.

## 5. Powers and Duties

Each Riding Association shall:

- recruit, welcome, and retain local members
- hold open local meetings and policy discussions
- elect delegates to conventions in accordance with Party rules
- assist in candidate search and nomination processes
- manage local fundraising and expenditures lawfully
- submit required reports to the National Council or Chief Agent
- maintain an orderly transition when local officers change

## 6. Candidate Nomination Role

### 6.1 Local Responsibility

The Riding Association is the primary local body for preparing candidate
nomination contests, identifying prospects, and supporting accessible member
participation.

### 6.2 National Rules

All nominations shall be conducted under national rules approved by the National
Council and in compliance with federal law.

### 6.3 Fairness Standards

Nomination meetings must use secret ballots, neutral administration, published
cut-off dates, and a clear appeal path.

## 7. Delegates and Policy Sponsorship

### 7.1 Convention Delegates

Each Riding Association may elect convention delegates under the Convention
System Bylaw.

### 7.2 Policy Sponsorship

A Riding Association may sponsor policy resolutions, constitutional amendments,
and bylaw proposals for national consideration.

## 8. Finances and Reporting

### 8.1 Financial Stewardship

Local finances shall be administered by the local Treasurer under the direction
of the executive and subject to all instructions of the Chief Agent.

### 8.2 Reporting

Each Riding Association shall submit:

- annual officer and contact information
- meeting dates and AGM outcomes
- financial reports required by the Party or by law
- any local constitution amendments

## 9. Support, Intervention, and Decertification

### 9.1 Support First

If a Riding Association is failing to function, the National Council should
first provide coaching, mediation, or organizational support.

### 9.2 Administration

The National Council may place a Riding Association under temporary
administration only where there is serious dysfunction, financial risk, legal
non-compliance, or loss of fair process.

### 9.3 Due Process

Except in urgent cases, the Riding Association must receive notice of the
concerns, an opportunity to respond, and written reasons for intervention.

### 9.4 Decertification

Decertification is a last resort and requires a two-thirds vote of the National
Council. A decertified Riding Association may reapply for recognition after
correcting the grounds for decertification.

## 10. Redistribution and Continuity

If federal electoral boundaries change, the National Council shall adopt a
transition plan for membership transfer, asset handling, and re-recognition so
local democratic continuity is preserved as far as practical.
