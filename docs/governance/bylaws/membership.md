## 1. Purpose

This bylaw defines who may join the Party, what rights membership carries, and
how membership status is protected, suspended, or ended.

## 2. Membership Structure

### 2.1 Single Voting Class

The Party shall maintain a single class of voting member known as a **Member in
Good Standing**. The Party may recognize supporters, caucuses, and commissions,
but those categories do not create separate voting classes unless the
constitution is amended.

### 2.2 Additional Designations

The following designations may be recorded for organizational purposes without
creating unequal voting rights:

- Youth Member: a Member in Good Standing under age 26
- Supporter: a non-member registrant who may receive updates and volunteer but
  may not vote or hold office
- Member of a recognized caucus, circle, or commission

## 3. Eligibility

### 3.1 Basic Requirements

An individual may become a Member in Good Standing if the individual:

- is at least 14 years of age
- is a Canadian citizen or permanent resident ordinarily resident in Canada
- affirms the Party's constitution, code of conduct, and political purpose
- is not knowingly a registered member in good standing of another registered
  federal political party (this restriction applies to officers and confirmed
  candidates for federal nomination; ordinary members who hold concurrent
  provincial or territorial party memberships are not affected)
- provides the information reasonably required to verify identity, residence,
  and riding assignment

### 3.2 No Mandatory Fee

Membership shall not require a mandatory fee. The Party may solicit voluntary
contributions, but inability or unwillingness to contribute money shall not
affect membership status.

### 3.3 Membership Term

Membership remains active for four years from the date of the most recent
affirmation or renewal unless ended earlier by resignation, death, suspension,
or expulsion.

## 4. Enrollment and Good Standing

### 4.1 Enrollment Methods

Applications may be submitted through secure digital or paper processes
approved by the National Council.

### 4.2 When Good Standing Begins

Membership begins when the application is accepted into the Party's official
membership registry.

### 4.3 Participation Windows

Unless a longer period is set by another bylaw:

- a new member may participate in regular meetings and local deliberations
  immediately after acceptance
- a new member may vote in local executive elections, convention delegate
  selections, and ordinary Party referenda after 14 days
- a new member may vote in candidate nomination contests or leadership contests
  only if the member joined at least 45 days before the membership cut-off for
  that contest

## 5. Rights of Members

Each Member in Good Standing has the right to:

- attend meetings of the member's Riding Association
- vote in Party processes for which the member is eligible
- seek election as a delegate or Party officer
- submit proposals under the policy and amendment processes
- receive reasonable notice of major votes and conventions
- access Party rules, minutes summaries, and official reports available to
  members
- receive procedural fairness in complaints and disciplinary matters

## 6. Duties of Members

Members shall:

- respect the constitution, bylaws, and adopted codes of conduct
- provide accurate membership information and keep contact details current
- act in good faith toward fellow members and Party institutions
- comply with campaign finance, privacy, and nomination rules when applicable
- refrain from harassment, discrimination, fraud, and intentional disruption of
  democratic processes

## 7. Caucuses, Circles, and Commissions

### 7.1 Recognized Formations

The National Convention or National Council may recognize member formations for
representation, organizing, or policy development, including:

- a Youth Commission
- an Indigenous Circle
- an Equity and Accessibility Commission

### 7.2 Purpose

Recognized formations may organize consultations, elect delegates where
authorized, and nominate representatives to Party bodies under the applicable
bylaw.

## 8. Membership Registry and Privacy

### 8.1 Official Register

The National Council shall maintain or supervise a single national membership
registry as the authoritative source for voting eligibility and riding
assignment.

### 8.2 Access Controls

Access to membership data must be limited to authorized Party officials and
campaign entities with a legitimate Party purpose, subject to privacy controls,
audit logging, and any legal requirements.

### 8.3 Member Access

Members may request correction of their own membership record and shall be told
the basis for any denial of voting eligibility.

## 9. Suspension, Expulsion, and Resignation

### 9.1 Resignation

A member may resign at any time by written notice or by using an approved
digital process.

### 9.2 Grounds for Suspension or Expulsion

A member may be suspended or expelled only for serious cause, including:

- material breach of Party rules
- fraud or financial misconduct
- harassment, discrimination, or threats
- deliberate sabotage of Party processes
- ineligibility discovered after admission

### 9.3 Interim Suspension

An interim suspension may be imposed where immediate action is needed to protect
member safety, preserve evidence, or maintain electoral compliance. Interim
suspensions must be reviewed promptly.

## 10. Discipline Procedure

### 10.1 Notice

The member must receive written notice of the allegation, the possible sanction,
and the process timeline.

### 10.2 Decision Maker

Complaints shall be heard by a Membership and Ethics Committee or other body
designated under the National Council Bylaw, provided that no decision maker has
a conflict of interest.

### 10.3 Right to Respond

The member shall have a reasonable opportunity to provide a written response and
to request an oral hearing where credibility or major rights are at stake.

### 10.4 Appeals

A member may appeal a final disciplinary decision to the Party's Appeals Panel
within 21 days. The Appeals Panel may uphold, vary, remit, or overturn the
decision.

## 11. Reporting

The National Council shall provide the National Convention with an annual
membership report summarizing growth, renewals, suspensions, appeals, and
regional participation trends, with personal information removed.
