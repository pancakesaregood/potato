## 1. Purpose

This bylaw defines the composition, powers, procedures, and accountability of
the National Council, the Party's governing body between National Conventions.

## 2. Composition

### 2.1 Voting Members

The National Council consists of the following voting members:

- President
- Vice-President for Organization
- Vice-President for Policy and Convention
- Secretary
- Treasurer
- one council representative elected from each province
- one council representative elected from each territory
- Leader
- Chair of the Parliamentary Caucus
- Chair of the Youth Commission
- Chair of the Indigenous Circle
- Chair of the Equity and Accessibility Commission

### 2.2 Non-Voting Participants

The following may attend without a vote unless separately elected to Council:

- Executive Director
- Chief Agent
- Ombudsperson
- legal counsel or advisors invited for specific matters

### 2.3 Terms

National officers serve until the close of the next regular National Convention
after their election. Provincial and territorial representatives serve two-year
terms according to their section rules.

## 3. Powers

The National Council shall:

- implement National Convention decisions
- supervise national finances, staffing, and administration
- maintain the membership registry and governance records
- recognize, support, or intervene in Riding Associations
- appoint committees, panels, and temporary working groups
- call conventions, leadership contests, and national referenda where
  authorized
- issue compliance directives to Party bodies
- publish regular summaries of decisions and reports to members

## 4. Limits

The National Council may not:

- amend the constitution
- make permanent bylaw changes outside the convention process
- override a valid convention decision except where later law requires a
  conflicting action

## 5. Officers

### 5.1 President

The President is the chief volunteer officer of the Party and chairs the
National Council.

### 5.2 Vice-President for Organization

The Vice-President for Organization oversees membership growth, field structure,
and Riding Association support.

### 5.3 Vice-President for Policy and Convention

The Vice-President for Policy and Convention coordinates the policy cycle,
convention planning, and related committees.

### 5.4 Secretary

The Secretary maintains minutes, notices, governance records, and official seals
or attestations.

### 5.5 Treasurer

The Treasurer oversees budgeting, internal financial reporting, and audit
liaison, subject to the legal authority of the Chief Agent.

## 6. Meetings and Transparency

### 6.1 Frequency

The National Council shall meet at least six times per year, with at least one
meeting designed for extended strategic review.

### 6.2 Notice

Regular meetings require at least seven days' notice. Emergency meetings may be
called on shorter notice where necessary.

### 6.3 Quorum

Quorum is 40 percent of voting members, including at least one national officer
and representatives from at least five provinces or territories.

### 6.4 Hybrid Participation

Meetings may be held in person, by teleconference, or in hybrid form.

### 6.5 Reporting

The Council shall publish a member-facing summary of each meeting within 30 days
except for confidential personnel, litigation, or disciplinary matters.

## 7. Committees and Panels

The National Council shall maintain or create the following bodies as needed:

- Finance and Compliance Committee
- Membership and Organizing Committee
- Policy and Convention Committee
- Governance, Ethics, and Appeals Committee
- Leadership Election Committee when a leadership process is active

The Council may also create temporary panels for nominations, reviews, or
special investigations.

## 8. Appeals Panel

### 8.1 Establishment

The National Council shall appoint an independent Appeals Panel of at least
three members who are not current voting members of the Council.

### 8.2 Jurisdiction

The Appeals Panel hears appeals arising under membership discipline, local
interventions, nominations, and any other process assigned by bylaw.

### 8.3 Powers

The Appeals Panel may uphold, vary, remit, or overturn the decision under
appeal and shall provide written reasons.

## 9. Conflict of Interest

Council members must disclose real or apparent conflicts of interest and abstain
from participation where required by policy, law, or a ruling of the chair.

## 10. Vacancies and Removal

### 10.1 Vacancies

If an elected Council seat becomes vacant, the relevant body may appoint an
interim replacement until the next election for that seat.

### 10.2 Removal

A Council member may be removed from office for serious cause by a two-thirds
vote of the National Council after notice and an opportunity to respond, unless
another document prescribes a higher threshold.

## 11. Provisional Bylaw Orders

Where the constitution permits, the National Council may adopt a provisional
bylaw order by a two-thirds vote. Such an order must:

- state the reason for urgency
- identify the specific text adopted
- specify the date it takes effect
- be circulated to members promptly
- be placed before the next National Convention for ratification

## 12. Annual Reporting

The National Council shall submit to each regular National Convention:

- an organizational report
- audited or reviewed financial statements as required
- a membership report
- a policy-cycle report
- a list of provisional orders, interventions, and appeals outcomes
