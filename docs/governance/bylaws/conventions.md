## 1. Purpose

This bylaw defines how the Party convenes, accredits, and conducts national
conventions.

## 2. Types of Convention

### 2.1 Regular National Convention

A regular National Convention shall be held every two years and shall include
governance, organizational, and policy business.

### 2.2 Special Convention

A Special Convention may be called by:

- a two-thirds vote of the National Council; or
- a petition from at least 25 recognized Riding Associations spanning at least
  five provinces or territories

### 2.3 Emergency Convention

An Emergency Convention may be called on shorter timelines where legal
compliance, leadership vacancy, merger, or other urgent national circumstances
require it.

## 3. Notice and Agenda

### 3.1 Notice

Regular National Conventions require at least 120 days' notice. Special
Conventions require at least 45 days' notice unless an emergency has been
declared by the National Council.

### 3.2 Agenda Publication

The proposed agenda, amendment package, and credential rules shall be published
at least 60 days before a regular National Convention.

## 4. Participation Classes

### 4.1 Registered Members

Any Member in Good Standing who registers by the published deadline may attend
the convention, speak as permitted by the chair, and vote on policy matters
assigned to the full registered membership.

### 4.2 Accredited Delegates

Accredited delegates vote on structural and constitutional matters, including:

- constitutional amendments
- bylaw amendments
- election of national officers
- merger or dissolution motions
- any other matter expressly assigned to delegates by the constitution or this
  bylaw

### 4.3 Observers and Guests

Observers, staff, media, and invited guests may attend on terms set by the
National Council.

## 5. Delegate Allocation

### 5.1 Riding Association Delegates

Each recognized Riding Association is entitled to delegates allocated
proportionally to its membership:

- 1 delegate as a base allotment
- 1 additional delegate for every 75 Members in Good Standing in the riding,
  up to a total of 10 delegates

No riding may receive fewer than 1 delegate. Geographic representation
across provinces and territories is served through the provincial and
territorial section delegates in section 5.2, not through an elevated
equal base allotment.

### 5.2 Other Delegate Sources

The following bodies may elect delegates:

- each provincial or territorial section: 2 delegates
- Youth Commission: 4 delegates
- Indigenous Circle: 4 delegates
- Equity and Accessibility Commission: 4 delegates
- Parliamentary Caucus: up to 10 delegates in total

### 5.3 Delegate Selection Standards

Delegate elections must use fair notice, secret ballots where contested, and
reasonable efforts toward gender balance, language inclusion, and accessibility.

## 6. Voting Authority

### 6.1 Member Votes

Policy resolutions, priority rankings, and leadership review questions may be
submitted to all registered members attending the convention through approved
in-person or secure remote voting methods.

### 6.2 Delegate Votes

Delegate-only voting applies to constitutional, bylaw, officer-election, and
structural governance matters.

## 7. Resolutions and Amendments

### 7.1 Deadlines

- constitutional amendments: 60 days before the convention
- bylaw amendments: 45 days before the convention
- ordinary policy resolutions: 45 days before the convention
- emergency resolutions: by the deadline set by the convention chair, subject
  to admission rules

### 7.2 Consolidation

The Policy and Convention Committee may consolidate substantially similar
resolutions, provided it does not change their intent without sponsor
consultation.

### 7.3 Emergency Resolution Admission

An emergency resolution may be admitted after the ordinary deadline only if:

- it concerns a development that arose after the deadline; and
- two-thirds of the Resolutions Committee or a simple majority of delegates
  agree to admit it

## 8. Quorum and Procedure

### 8.1 Quorum

Convention quorum is reached when at least half of accredited delegates have
been credentialed and delegates from at least five provinces or territories are
present or connected through approved remote participation.

### 8.2 Rules of Order

The National Council shall publish procedural rules before the convention. The
chair may interpret those rules, subject to appeal to the delegates.

### 8.3 Hybrid and Remote Participation

The Party shall use secure technology to allow remote attendance and voting
where feasible, especially for members in remote regions or with accessibility
needs.

## 9. Elections at Convention

National officers elected at convention shall be chosen by ranked ballot of
accredited delegates unless the convention adopts another voting method for a
specific uncontested office.

## 10. Convention Committees

The National Council shall establish convention committees, including:

- Credentials
- Rules and Procedure
- Resolutions
- Elections
- Accessibility and Interpretation

## 11. Records

The Party shall publish convention decisions, officer election results, adopted
amendments, and policy outcomes within 30 days after the close of convention.
