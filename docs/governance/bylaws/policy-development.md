## 1. Purpose

This bylaw creates a structured, member-driven process for developing, debating,
adopting, reviewing, and updating Party policy.

## 2. Policy Principles

Party policy development shall be:

- member-initiated
- regionally inclusive
- transparent in drafting and amendment
- evidence-informed
- fiscally and legally aware
- open to revision as conditions change

## 3. Policy Bodies

### 3.1 National Policy Committee

The National Council shall appoint a National Policy Committee to manage the
policy cycle, consolidate resolutions where needed, and publish the draft policy
book for convention consideration.

### 3.2 Local and Affiliated Inputs

Riding Associations, recognized commissions, the Parliamentary Caucus, and
member working groups may all contribute to policy development.

## 4. Eligible Sponsors

The following may submit policy proposals:

- any recognized Riding Association
- any recognized commission, circle, or caucus
- the National Council
- the Parliamentary Caucus
- any group of at least 10 Members in Good Standing from at least 3 ridings

## 5. Types of Policy Submission

The Party recognizes four main policy submission types:

- Policy Resolution: a specific substantive policy proposal
- Platform Directive: a near-term electoral priority
- Discussion Paper: a proposal for consultation rather than immediate adoption
- Emergency Resolution: a late-breaking proposal responding to new events

## 6. Annual Policy Cycle

### 6.1 Intake

Policy intake remains open year-round through a national submission system.

### 6.2 Sponsor Verification

The National Policy Committee or its staff support shall verify sponsor
eligibility and basic formatting within 30 days where practical.

### 6.3 Screening

The National Policy Committee may flag duplication, legal conflicts, federal
jurisdiction issues, fiscal concerns, or direct contradictions with the
constitution. It may recommend edits or consolidation but may not suppress a
properly sponsored proposal without written reasons.

### 6.4 Publication

A convention policy booklet containing all admitted proposals, committee
recommendations, and minority notes shall be published at least 45 days before a
regular National Convention.

### 6.5 Member Prioritization

Registered convention members may rank policy priorities before the convention
to determine plenary debate order.

## 7. Convention Consideration

### 7.1 Debate

Policy resolutions shall be debated through a combination of policy workshops,
regional caucuses, and plenary votes.

### 7.2 Voting

Unless the convention adopts a higher threshold for a specific category, policy
resolutions are adopted by a simple majority of valid votes cast by registered
convention members.

### 7.3 Amendments from the Floor

Convention amendments that remain within the scope of the original proposal may
be accepted by the chair and voted on at convention.

## 8. Policy Book and Maintenance

### 8.1 Official Policy Book

Adopted policy resolutions shall be recorded in a versioned Party policy book
maintained by the National Policy Committee.

### 8.2 Classification

The policy book shall identify whether a policy is:

- standing policy
- time-limited directive
- election platform priority
- archived or superseded

### 8.3 Sunset Review

Standing policies shall be reviewed after three regular convention cycles unless
the convention specifies otherwise.

## 9. Emergency Resolutions

An Emergency Resolution may be submitted after the normal deadline by the
National Council, the Parliamentary Caucus, a recognized commission, or at least
50 members. It may be admitted only under the rules of the Convention System
Bylaw.

## 10. Platform Formation and Accountability

### 10.1 Platform Formation

The election platform shall be prepared by the Leader and campaign team in
consultation with the National Council and relevant policy bodies.

### 10.2 Presumption of Consistency

The platform should reflect the adopted policy book unless urgent electoral,
legal, fiscal, or coalition considerations require a departure.

### 10.3 Variance Report

Within 120 days after a federal general election, the National Council shall
publish a member-facing report explaining any material departures between the
platform and the adopted policy book.
