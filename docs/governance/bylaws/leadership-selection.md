## 1. Purpose

This bylaw governs leadership vacancies, leadership contests, campaign rules,
balloting, and leadership review.

## 2. Vacancy and Interim Leadership

### 2.1 Vacancy

The leadership becomes vacant on resignation, death, permanent incapacity,
successful leadership review against the Leader, or any other event recognized
by law.

### 2.2 Interim Leader

Within 14 days of a vacancy, the National Council shall appoint an Interim
Leader after consulting the Parliamentary Caucus. The Interim Leader may serve
until the new Leader is declared.

### 2.3 Interim Leader Candidacy

An Interim Leader may seek the permanent leadership only if the Interim Leader
resigns that interim office before the close of nominations.

## 3. Timing of a Leadership Contest

### 3.1 Contest Call

The National Council shall issue a formal contest call within 30 days of a
leadership vacancy.

### 3.2 Ballot Timing

The final leadership ballot shall occur within 210 days of the vacancy unless a
general election, legal restriction, or force majeure makes that timeline
impracticable.

## 4. Leadership Election Committee

The National Council shall appoint an independent Leadership Election Committee
to administer the contest. The committee shall publish the contest calendar,
candidate rules, spending cap, debate schedule, and ballot procedures.

## 5. Candidate Eligibility

To become an official leadership candidate, a person must:

- be a Member in Good Standing for at least 12 months before nominations close,
  unless waived for the founding cycle by the National Council
- submit at least 500 valid supporting signatures from members
- include signatures from at least 7 provinces or territories, with at least 20
  from each of those jurisdictions
- agree to all financial reporting, data-use, and campaign rules
- pay a refundable compliance deposit of CAD 1,000; candidates who demonstrate
  financial hardship through a standardized form established by the National
  Council shall pay a reduced deposit of CAD 100, which is non-discretionary
  once hardship criteria are met

## 6. Campaign Rules

### 6.1 Fair Access

Every certified candidate must receive equal access to Party-approved membership
contact channels and membership data tools on uniform terms.

### 6.2 Spending Cap

The Leadership Election Committee shall set and publish a spending cap before
nominations open.

### 6.3 Fundraising and Compliance

Campaigns may raise and spend funds only in compliance with federal law and
Party rules. Required reports must be filed on time.

### 6.4 Debates

The Party shall organize at least three official debates, including at least one
substantially bilingual debate and at least one event designed for remote
participation.

## 7. Voter Eligibility

Only Members in Good Standing who joined the Party at least 45 days before the
published membership cut-off may vote in a leadership contest.

## 8. Voting Method

### 8.1 Ranked Ballot

Leadership votes shall use a ranked ballot.

### 8.2 Equal Riding Weight

Each federal electoral district shall be assigned 100 points. The points for a
riding are allocated proportionally among candidates based on the valid ballots
cast by eligible members in that riding.

### 8.3 Counting

If no candidate wins more than 50 percent of total national points on the first
count, the candidate with the fewest points is eliminated and the ballots are
redistributed until one candidate secures a majority of points.

### 8.4 Tie Breaking

Any tie affecting elimination or the final result is resolved first by total raw
ballots cast nationally and then by lot if still tied.

## 9. Ballot Administration

Voting may occur by secure online, mail, telephone-assisted, or in-person means
approved by the Leadership Election Committee, provided the method protects
secrecy, verification, and accessibility.

## 10. Leadership Review

### 10.1 Regular Review

At the first regular National Convention following a federal general election,
all registered convention members shall vote on whether to continue the Leader
in office.

### 10.2 Threshold

The Leader is continued in office only if at least 60 percent of valid votes
support continuation.

### 10.3 Triggered Review

The National Council may order a membership-wide leadership review by a
two-thirds vote, or shall do so upon receiving a petition from at least 20
percent of recognized Riding Associations spanning at least five provinces or
territories.

## 11. Complaints and Appeals

Decisions of the Leadership Election Committee may be appealed to the Appeals
Panel on an expedited basis. The Appeals Panel may issue binding directions
where required to preserve fairness or compliance.
