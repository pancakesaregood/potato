# Article 14: Ten Year Vision

## Summary

A ten-year vision is not a promise. It is a planning horizon — a way of
connecting immediate policy choices to long-range goals, so that decisions
made now are evaluated against where they lead, not just whether they are
popular this quarter.

The PPPC commits to publishing a ten-year national roadmap at each governance
cycle, maintaining it as a live document rather than a static declaration,
and holding it to the same transparency standards as every other governance
output: reasons documented, changes visible, progress measurable.

This article defines the structure and governance of that ten-year vision.
The substance of the vision is drawn from the full manifesto — housing,
climate, equality, democracy, reconciliation, fiscal responsibility — and
updated as those articles evolve. Article 14 is the container; the other
articles are the content.

---

## Principles

**1. Long-range planning must survive elections.**
Policy that requires ten years to work cannot be designed for four-year
cycles. The ten-year roadmap is explicitly designed to span multiple
governments — including governments led by parties other than the PPPC.
The goal is to articulate what durable progress looks like, so that
any government can be held to account for movement toward it.

**2. Milestones must be specific and measurable.**
"A more equitable Canada by 2035" is not a milestone — it is a sentiment.
Milestones are specific, quantified, and attributable: percentage reductions
in measurable gaps, completion dates for infrastructure programmes, legislative
targets, and indicator thresholds. If a milestone cannot be measured, it
is not a milestone.

**3. Revision is a feature, not a failure.**
The roadmap will be revised. Circumstances change. Evidence updates.
Political coalitions shift. A vision document that cannot be revised
is a monument, not a plan. Revisions are tracked in the governance
repository with reasons documented. What changed, why, and who decided
is part of the permanent record.

**4. Scenario planning is mandatory.**
A ten-year vision built on a single optimistic scenario is planning for
luck. The roadmap includes at minimum: a base case, a constrained case
(slower growth, higher climate impacts, more political resistance), and
an accelerated case (strong coalition, favourable conditions). Major
milestones are evaluated against all three scenarios.

**5. The vision belongs to citizens, not to the party.**
The PPPC does not hold a monopoly on ten-year planning for Canada. The
roadmap is a contribution to a public conversation, openly published,
open to critique, and explicitly inviting of alternative visions. It is
authoritative for the PPPC's governance work; it is not authoritative
for anyone else.

## Policy Mechanisms

**Ten-year roadmap publication**
The PPPC publishes a ten-year national roadmap at each regular governance
cycle, structured around the following themes drawn from manifesto articles:
- Democratic health and governance quality (Articles 01, 02, 08)
- Equality, rights, and civil liberties (Articles 03, 04, 11)
- Material wellbeing: housing, health, income (Articles 05, 07, 09)
- Ecological sustainability (Articles 06, 10)
- Reconciliation (Article 12)
- Canada's international role (Article 16)

Each theme has: a ten-year headline goal, three-to-five specific measurable
milestones with dates, baseline data, and data sources.

**Milestone dashboard**
The roadmap milestones are maintained in a live public dashboard linked
from the party's governance repository. The dashboard is updated at minimum
quarterly, shows current status against each milestone, and links to the
data source for each indicator. Where data is unavailable to update a
milestone, the reason is noted and a data acquisition plan is published.

**Two-year review cycle**
At each two-year governance review:
- Progress against milestones is assessed and published
- Milestones that are ahead of, on, or behind schedule are identified
- Milestones that are no longer achievable or relevant are revised or
  retired with documented reasons
- New milestones may be added consistent with the framework above
- Scenario assumptions are reviewed against current conditions

**Scenario review**
At each governance cycle, an external scenario review — conducted with
at least two subject matter experts not employed by the PPPC — examines:
- Whether the base-case scenario assumptions remain valid
- What the current constrained-case trajectory looks like
- Key uncertainties and decision forks that the next two years will resolve

Scenario review outputs are published in the cycle report.

**Contribution from outside the party**
The ten-year roadmap is published in the governance repository and is
open to public contribution through the standard proposal process. Policy
proposals that explicitly engage with the roadmap — including proposals
to revise milestones, add new themes, or dispute the evidence base for
existing milestones — are welcomed and prioritised for review. The roadmap
is not a closed document.

---

## Measurable Outcomes

- Ten-year roadmap published with the first governance cycle after
  this article's adoption, with all required components present.
- Milestone dashboard is live and updated quarterly.
- Two-year review reports are published on the expected schedule,
  with milestone status clearly documented.
- Scenario review is conducted and published at every governance cycle.
- At least one milestone in each theme area has published baseline
  data and is trackable against a defined data source within one year.
- Revisions to the roadmap are tracked in version history with
  documented reasons, accessible to any member of the public.
