# Article 11: Freelander Citizenship

## Summary

This article defines a placeholder concept for portable civic affiliation and digital participation that does not replace Canadian citizenship.

## Principles

- Any future freelander model must respect Canadian law and constitutional rights.
- Digital civic status should expand participation without creating a second-class system.
- Identity design should prioritize privacy, consent, and clear legal boundaries.

## Policy Mechanisms

- Commission a legal and technical review before any operational model is adopted.
- Limit early use to consultation, education, and pilot civic participation tools.
- Require explicit parliamentary and public review before any legal status is created.

## Measurable Outcomes

- A public options paper is produced before implementation decisions are made.
- Any pilot clearly states its legal limits and participant safeguards.
- Privacy, identity, and access risks are documented and reviewed openly.
