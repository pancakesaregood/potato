# Article 15: Child Support Smart Contracts

## Summary

This article explores how automation tools could improve child-support administration while preserving judicial oversight and privacy.

## Principles

- The best interests of the child must remain the governing standard.
- Automation should support enforcement and transparency, not remove human appeal rights.
- Family law technology must be interoperable, secure, and reversible.

## Policy Mechanisms

- Pilot digital escrow and rule-based payment tracking for child-support cases.
- Require judicial override, human review, and audited exception handling.
- Integrate notification, recordkeeping, and payment status into a secure workflow.

## Measurable Outcomes

- Payment processing times and arrears recovery improve in pilot programs.
- Error rates, appeals, and overrides are tracked publicly in aggregate form.
- Participants report better clarity about obligations and payment status.
