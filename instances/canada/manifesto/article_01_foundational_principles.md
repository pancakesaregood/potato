# Article 01: Foundational Principles

## Summary

This article defines the base political values of the Peoples Potato Party
of Canada (PPPC) and provides a stable interpretive frame for all policy
work. These principles are not policy positions — they are the commitments
that every policy position must be consistent with. When two proposals
conflict, the foundational principles provide the basis for resolution.

The PPPC operates as a governance-as-code project: its documents are
version-controlled, changes are proposed and reviewed openly, and positions
evolve through documented iterative cycles rather than closed internal
processes. This is not a technical choice — it is a direct expression of
the values in this article. A party that believes in transparency governs
itself transparently. See Article 02 for the full governance model.

---

## Principles

**1. Human dignity is non-negotiable.**
Every person has inherent worth that is not conditional on productivity,
citizenship status, political alignment, or any other variable. Policy
that treats some people as expendable — even in pursuit of aggregate
gains — is incompatible with this party's values.

**2. Evidence informs policy; ideology does not replace it.**
The PPPC holds political values, but those values do not predetermine
conclusions on factual questions. Where evidence is strong, policy follows
it. Where evidence is contested or absent, policy acknowledges uncertainty
rather than manufacturing false confidence.

**3. Democratic participation is a practice, not a ceremony.**
Elections are a floor, not a ceiling. Citizens should be able to engage
with policy development continuously — proposing changes, reviewing
decisions, and holding institutions accountable between elections. The
PPPC's open governance model is a demonstration of this principle in
practice.

**4. Regional and demographic balance is a design requirement.**
Canada is not a uniform country. Federal policy that works well for one
region, demographic, or community while failing others is not a neutral
policy — it is a policy with beneficiaries and costs that must be
identified and justified. Balance is a design constraint, not an
afterthought.

**5. Transparency is structural, not performative.**
Transparency means decisions made through documented processes, reasons
published and accessible, records retained and retrievable, and corrections
made visibly when decisions turn out to be wrong. The party holds itself
to this standard and applies the same standard to government.

**6. Accountability requires consequence.**
Rules without enforcement are suggestions. Accountability means that when
obligations are not met, there are defined and proportionate consequences —
for institutions, officers, and the party itself.

## Policy Mechanisms

**Public principles register**
This article and its full version history form the public principles
register. Every major policy proposal must cite the foundational principle
it serves. Proposals that cannot be traced to a foundational principle are
returned to authors for revision before entering formal review.

**Plain-language rationale requirements**
Every amendment to a manifesto article or governance document must include
a plain-language rationale note explaining what changed, why it changed,
and which foundational principle it serves. Rationale notes are part of
the permanent governance record and are published alongside each change.

**Foundational review at national conventions**
The foundational principles are reviewed at every regular national
convention. Reviews may result in amendments subject to the constitutional
amendment process, or in reaffirmation. The outcome of each review —
including minority views — is published in the cycle report.

**Open governance as demonstration**
The party maintains its manifesto and governance documents in a public,
version-controlled repository. Proposed changes are submitted as pull
requests, reviewed openly, validated by automated checks, and merged only
when consensus is reached. This makes the party's policy development
process legible to any member of the public — not just party insiders.

---

## Measurable Outcomes

- Every major policy proposal in a governance cycle cites the foundational
  principle it serves, tracked in the cycle report.
- Convention review of foundational principles occurs on the published
  schedule, with outcomes and participation rates reported publicly.
- Plain-language rationale notes are present for all merged policy changes
  within two governance cycles of this article's adoption.
- The governance repository's public contribution rate — proposals from
  non-founding contributors — increases cycle over cycle.
- Members can trace any current policy position back to its originating
  proposal and the principle it cited.
