# Article 18 — Directed Taxation and Fiscal Representation

## Summary

Taxation is the mechanism by which Canadians collectively fund the society
they share. But the current system gives citizens no meaningful voice in
how their contributions are allocated. A person who opposes military
spending funds it anyway. A person who believes scientific research is
the highest public investment has no way to act on that belief through
the tax system.

This article proposes directed taxation: a mechanism by which Canadians
may designate a portion of their tax contribution to specific budget
envelopes. The principle is straightforward — taxation becomes a form of
representation. Where your money goes reflects your priorities, not only
those of the government of the day.

This mechanism is available to all Canadian taxpayers. For Freelanders
(Article 11) who wish to contribute to specific public functions without
re-enrolling in the full tax system, directed taxation provides a
voluntary partial re-engagement path. A Freelander who wants to fund
cancer research, or environmental programs, or infrastructure in their
region, can do so — on their own terms, directed entirely by their own
allocation.

---

## Principles

**1. Taxation can be a form of democratic voice.**
Requiring citizens to fund activities they fundamentally oppose — with
no mechanism for dissent short of full non-compliance — weakens the
legitimacy of the tax system. Directed taxation does not eliminate
collective funding; it introduces a layer of genuine individual
representation within it.

**2. Direction must be meaningful, not symbolic.**
A directed taxation system that allows citizens to label their
contributions without those designations affecting real allocation
is not representation — it is performance. Directed funds must flow
to the designated envelopes and be tracked and reported publicly.

**3. Some functions remain collectively funded without direction.**
Not every budget item is appropriate for individual direction. Debt
servicing, treaty obligations, constitutional transfers to provinces,
and core democratic institutions (elections, courts, Parliament) are
funded from the common pool. These cannot be defunded by individual
non-allocation. The directed portion applies only to discretionary
federal spending envelopes.

**4. Simplicity of participation is a design requirement.**
If the allocation mechanism is complex enough that only financially
literate or highly engaged citizens can use it effectively, it
reproduces existing inequalities in civic voice. The interface must
be simple enough to complete in minutes, with plain-language
descriptions of each envelope.

**5. Freelander voluntary contributions are unconditional.**
A Freelander who makes a directed contribution does so voluntarily.
Their contribution is accepted, directed as specified, and
acknowledged. It does not change their Freelander status, does not
create future tax obligations, and does not entitle them to services
outside what their status already provides.

## Policy Mechanisms

**Directed allocation on annual tax filing**
All Canadian taxpayers may complete a Directed Allocation Schedule
as part of their annual federal tax filing. The schedule allows
them to designate up to 50% of their net federal tax payable across
available budget envelopes. The remaining 50% flows to the general
consolidated revenue fund as under current arrangements.

Available envelopes for direction include, but are not limited to:
- Scientific and medical research
- Environmental protection and restoration
- Housing and infrastructure
- Public healthcare and mental health
- Education and skills training
- Indigenous services and reconciliation
- International aid and development
- Arts, culture, and heritage

Envelopes are defined by Parliament and reviewed on a four-year
cycle. New envelopes may be proposed through public petition with
a defined signature threshold.

**Allocation that equals representation**
The federal government publishes, within 90 days of each fiscal
year-end, a full accounting of directed allocations received by
envelope — showing total directed amounts, percentage of total
discretionary spend each envelope received through direction, and
how directed funds were applied within each envelope. This report
is tabled in Parliament.

**Unallocated portions**
Where a taxpayer completes no Directed Allocation Schedule, or
allocates less than the maximum permitted 50%, the unallocated
portion flows to the general consolidated revenue fund. There is
no penalty for non-direction and no default forced allocation.

**Freelander voluntary contributions**
A registered Freelander (Article 11) may make a voluntary directed
contribution at any time through the same allocation mechanism.
Contributions are accepted in any amount above a minimum threshold
of $100 to manage administrative overhead. The Freelander specifies
the envelope. The contribution is processed, receipted, and included
in the public allocation report. It does not alter Freelander status
or create any ongoing obligation.

**Minimum contribution threshold for general taxpayers**
To prevent administrative overhead from fragmenting the system,
directed allocations must be at least $50 per envelope. A taxpayer
may direct to as many or as few envelopes as they choose, subject
to this minimum per envelope.

**Parliamentary accountability**
The Minister of Finance is required to respond, in a tabled
document, to significant divergences between directed allocation
patterns and the government's proposed budget — specifically where
an envelope receives substantially more or less directed funding
than the government's own allocation. This does not bind the
government's budget decisions, but it creates a public record
of the gap between citizen priorities and government priorities.

## Measurable Outcomes

- Directed Allocation Schedule available to all Canadian taxpayers
  within two years of this article's adoption as PPPC policy.
- Full public allocation report published within 90 days of each
  fiscal year-end, tabled in Parliament, and available in
  machine-readable format.
- Freelander voluntary contribution mechanism operational
  concurrent with the Directed Allocation Schedule launch.
- Participation rate in directed allocation tracked annually and
  published; target of 30% taxpayer participation within five
  years of launch.
- Ministerial response to allocation divergence tabled within
  60 days of each annual allocation report.
- Independent review of envelope definitions and allocation
  outcomes at four years, with public findings and a 90-day
  comment period before any envelope changes are made.
