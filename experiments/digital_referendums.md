## Policy Question

**Should Canadian citizens be able to initiate binding referendums through a
verified digital platform, and if so, what safeguards should exist?**

Canada currently has no federal citizen-initiated referendum mechanism. Federal
referendums have been held (1942, 1992) but only at the government's discretion.
Many democracies use citizen petitions to trigger binding or consultative votes
on specific questions.

This experiment asks: could a well-designed digital system make referendums
more accessible without making them easier to manipulate?

---

## Why This Is Difficult

This is not a simple yes/no question. The tradeoffs are real:

**Arguments for accessible digital referendums**
- Direct participation on specific issues, outside of election cycles
- Higher engagement on issues where citizens feel unrepresented
- Transparency: the question, the result, and the turnout are all public

**Arguments against, or for caution**
- Referendum questions are easily written to favour one outcome
- Turnout varies by demographic — digital access is not universal
- Well-funded campaigns can dominate the information environment
- Simple majorities can harm minorities in ways parliamentary debate prevents
- The 1992 Charlottetown referendum showed that complex constitutional
  questions are poorly suited to yes/no votes

Neither side is obviously wrong. The question is whether good design can
manage these tradeoffs — or whether they are fundamental.

## Debate Space

This experiment is open for structured contribution. You can contribute by:

1. **Opening a Policy Question issue** to raise a specific dimension of this
   debate (e.g., "What should the signature threshold be for triggering a
   referendum?")

2. **Opening a Policy Proposal PR** to propose a specific design element
   (e.g., a Citizens' Assembly model that interprets referendum outcomes rather
   than implementing them directly)

3. **Commenting on this file's PR history** to debate design choices

Questions worth debating:

- What threshold of signatures should trigger a referendum? (1% of eligible
  voters? 5%? Regional minimums?)
- Should the result be binding, consultative, or binding-unless-overridden
  by a supermajority in Parliament?
- What body writes the official question? Who reviews it for neutrality?
- How do you handle the digital access gap — what is the paper/in-person
  alternative?
- Should some subject areas (constitutional changes, minority rights,
  international treaties) be excluded?
- What is the minimum national turnout for a result to be valid?

## How to Propose a Solution

Copy `templates/proposal_template.md` to
`proposals/digital-referendums-[your-short-title].md`.

In your proposal, address at minimum:
- the specific mechanism you are proposing
- how it handles the access and manipulation risks described above
- what constitutional changes (if any) would be required
- how results would be implemented or ignored

You do not need to solve every problem. A proposal that addresses one specific
design question well is more useful than one that tries to resolve everything.
